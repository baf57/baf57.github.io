Controlling the Surface Properties of Binary Polymer Brush Coated Colloids via Target Nanoparticles.

Ozmaian, M., Freitas, B., & Coalson, R.

An investigation into how the surface properties of a polymer coated colloid can be changed by the presence of nanoparticles near the colloid. This was done via ball-and-spring course grained atomic simulation. A more in-depth description of this project can be found here in the blog section of this website as it was my senior thesis. The title of this section leads to the journal hosting the paper.

Docking Multiple Proteins Into Botulinum Toxin Type A Using Computational Methods.

Freitas, B.

An investigation into potential small inhibitors to Botulinum Toxin Type A. The small inhibitors were tested for inhibition strength via mixed atomic and course grained simulations. Of the 100 proposed inhibitors, 88 were ruled out as possibly useful inhibitors and the other 12 were later synthesized by other research groups to be investigated further.
