Thesis

Toward Improving General Quantum Imaging Algorithms and the Realization of Quantum Recording.

Ozmaian, M., Freitas, B., & Coalson, R.

An extensive thesis which works towards the development of a generalized theory for quantum imaging, supports this theory with the design, execution, and analysis of a quantum imaging scheme, and depicts the realization of quantum recording for the first time. This thesis is the culmination of the 2 years of work I conducted during my Master's degree at the University of Ottawa. This thesis was nominated by my defense committee for a Best Thesis in the Sciences Award. My thesis committee consisted of Dr.Hang Chi and Dr.Khabat Heshami, with Dr.Robert W. Boyd as my committee head, alongside my advisors Dr.Jeff Lundeen and Dr.Ben Sussman.

Papers

Quantum correlated image recording through noisy and turbulent channels.

Freitas, B., Zhang, Y., England, D.G., Lundeen, J.S., Sussman, B.J.

The development and demonstration of quantum image recording through noisy and turbulent channel. This novel technique allows for recordings utilizing quantum imaging at frame rates of around 5 frames per second, with noted advantages over traditional imaging and recording.

Controlling the Surface Properties of Binary Polymer Brush Coated Colloids via Target Nanoparticles.

Ozmaian, M., Freitas, B., & Coalson, R.

An investigation into how the surface properties of a polymer coated colloid can be changed by the presence of nanoparticles near the colloid. This was done via ball-and-spring course grained atomic simulation.

Increasing the Robustness of Quantum Correlated Imaging to Scattering.

Freitas B., Zhang Y., England D., Lundeen J., Sussman B.

An experimental design which utilized the correlations between photons generated via SPDC in order to retrieve corrected ghost images of targets through scattering. In opposition to previous methods, the measurement for this system utilizes a time tagging single photon detector array called the TPX3Cam. Presented at Photonics North 2023. Docking Multiple Proteins Into Botulinum Toxin Type A Using Computational Methods.

Freitas B.

An investigation into potential small inhibitors to Botulinum Toxin Type A. The small inhibitors were tested for inhibition strength via mixed atomic and course grained simulations. Of the 100 proposed inhibitors, 88 were ruled out as possibly useful inhibitors and the other 12 were later synthesized by other research groups to be investigated further.
