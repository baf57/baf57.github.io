Shoe Bench

Shown here is a collaboration project I did with one of my roommates to create a shoe bench for our entryway. (A quick note: the window molding above this bench is NOT level with the floor, and should not be used as a straight edge reference when viewing these photos).

I made this bench out of walnut for a few reasons:

  1. The currently existing furniture in our living room is made of dark wood
  2. Since shoes will be going on it the dark wood will help to hide any mud and scuffs
  3. I like the look and smell of walnut wood :)

The bench was constructed using only jointing, primarily half-lap and through joints. The bench has been tested to be able to hold over 500 pounds in weight with no trouble at all.

This bench is an on-going collaboration. I made the base, while my roommate is going to upholster the top part of the bench with memory foam and a green textile fabric. As of now my roommate has not completed upholstering the bench top, and so an unfinished board sits atop the bench in the meantime.

This bench was my first big project after the board game shelf, and took me only around 20 hours to complete. This was my first time working with undimensioned lumber, and gave me a lot of practice with squaring and resawing.
