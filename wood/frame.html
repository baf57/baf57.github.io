Juniper Tree Frame

This is a frame that I made for a print I purchased from Moab Made in Moab, Utah. The print is a line drawing of a juniper tree, many of which can be found in the desert mountains surrounding Moab.

I used cherry wood as the primary frame wood since the sienna color is similar to that of the desert landscape the trees are found in.

The corner reinforcements are done in walnut. The walnut is reminiscent of the dark exterior of the juniper tree. In order to accentuate the twisted nature of the juniper tree, I opted on a crossing configuration for the corner reinforcements. I decided to round the corners, as this would make the twisted appearance even more apparent. This further accentuates the flowing nature of the tree.

This is the first custom frame I have ever made, and I am very happy with how it turned out. I learned a lot making this frame, and I found it a very rewarding process. I created a corner reinforcement jig in order to make this frame. Overall, between making the jig, cutting the wood, finishing the wood, as well as waiting for the glue to dry, this frame took be about 8 hours to complete.
