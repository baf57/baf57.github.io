Board Game Shelf

Introduction

Take me right to the pictures!



This is a shelf for boardgames which I built for my roommate and I in order to hold our ever-growing board game collection.

Design

I started with researching standard board game box sizes, while also measuring the boxes of the board games we already had. This allowed me to come up with the shelf sizes.

I knew I wanted to make the frame out of oak boards and the shelves out of oak plywood. I created a 3D model to determine how I would layout the cuts on the plywood sheet.

There are some aspects of this design which I would change if I were to ever make it again. Firstly, I would use lapped joints rather than butted joints. This would lead to more structural stability, and would likely negate the need for reinforcement with screws. I would also change the method of shelf support from sitting on wood rails (highlighted above, left), to instead slot into the frame. This would increase the strength of the shelves as a whole, and would also be cleaner. Lastly, I would make sure that the height of the shelves includes the lip which hangs down at the top of each opening (highlighted above, right). Better yet, I could not include that lip at all. The lip was included to hide the wooden rails the shelves sit on, however using the aforementioned shelf support system, they would not be needed.

Finished Product

The shelf stands at 6 feet tall and it made of an oak frame with oak veneered plywood shelves.

The shelves are primarily jointed together using butted joints reinforced by pocket screws. The shelves are very stable and strong, having now successfully held around 1000 pounds of frequently shifted weight for over 6 months now.

This is still the largest single project I have done so far. In total, this project took around 80 hours to complete. Since this was my first real project, this included not only the time to work on the shelves themselves, but also the time to create many jigs, learn and practice many different techniques, and also perform some on-the-fly redesign.
